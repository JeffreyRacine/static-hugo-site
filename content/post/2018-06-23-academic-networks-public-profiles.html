Knowledge transfer and the dissemination of research is often afforded no more than an afterthought, yet it is a crucial component of the academic mission.

I strive to impress the often overlooked importance of replicability, automated backup procedures, and version control on my students. But dissemination of work at conferences, being visible, and in general “marketing” our research often gets short shrift.

It is a shame when one spends months, perhaps years engaged in a research project yet no one is aware of the work, even when it eventually appears in print. “If a tree falls…” comes to mind.

The past decade has witnessed exponential growth in the number of social media outlets for the general public. Similar growth has occurred for academic social media outlets and hosting platforms. Some of the more popular in my field include

  • IDEAS/RePEc

    IDEAS is the largest bibliographic database dedicated to Economics and available freely on the Internet, based on RePEc. RePEc is a large volunteer effort to enhance the free dissemination of research in Economics which includes bibliographic metadata from over 2,000 participating archives, including all the major publishers and research outlets.

  • GitHub

    GitHub is a development platform inspired by the way you work. From open source to business, you can host and review code, manage projects, and build software alongside millions of other developers.

  • Google Scholar

    Google Scholar provides a simple way to broadly search for scholarly literature. Google Scholar Citations provide a simple way for authors to keep track of citations to their articles. You can check who is citing your publications, graph citations over time, and compute several citation metrics.

  • ResearchGate

    ResearchGate is a social networking site for scientists and researchers to share papers, ask and answer questions, and find collaborators.

  • Mendeley

    Mendeley is a free reference manager and an academic social network

  • ORCID

    ORCID provides a persistent digital identifier that distinguishes you from every other researcher and, through integration in key research workflows such as manuscript and grant submission, supports automated linkages between you and your professional activities ensuring that your work is recognized.

It doesn’t take much effort to create a profile and add some basic information on your academic status, education, research interests, and organize your publications. Even though there may be some overlap among the services, by creating each of these profiles you can give the visibility of your research a boost.
